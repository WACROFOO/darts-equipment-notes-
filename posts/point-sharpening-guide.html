How to Sharpen Dart Points: The Shape That Works

By TungstenTalk | March 2026

I see the same advice everywhere: "sharpen your points." Nobody explains what shape you are aiming for. The answer matters because the wrong shape causes more bounce-outs than a dull point.

The three tip shapes

Needle-sharp: pricks your finger like a pin. Too sharp. When it hits blade wire, it digs in and stops dead. The dart bounces straight back.

Flat/blunt: visibly rounded. Too blunt. Cannot push past the wire into the sisal.

Gentle dome: smooth, slightly rounded. Does not prick your finger but you feel a distinct point. This is correct. The dome slides along the wire surface and finds the sisal gap beside it.

How to get the dome shape

Hold the dart at 30 degrees to a sharpening stone or 150-grit sandpaper. Rotate slowly while drawing the point across. Ten seconds per dart.

Check by running your thumb across the tip. Should feel smooth with slight texture, not glassy. The tip should catch your skin gently without pricking.

How often

Every 2-3 weeks if you play 3+ times per week. Points wear down gradually from hitting wire. You will not notice the decline until you sharpen and suddenly everything sticks better.

A dart sharpener costs about three pounds and lasts years. The cylindrical ones with a hole in the centre are easiest.

The test

Sharpen one dart. Throw it alongside two unsharpened darts. If the sharpened one bounces less and sticks more consistently, your points needed maintenance. Sharpen the other two.

Thirty seconds, zero ongoing cost. Directly reduces bounce-outs. No reason not to do it.

Sharpening stones are available at Darts Corner, Red Dragon, and most dart retailers for a few pounds. The PDC pros sharpen before every match - if it matters at that level, it matters at yours.
