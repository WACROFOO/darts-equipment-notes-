Barrel Diameter by Tungsten Percentage: Actual Measurements

By TungstenTalk | March 2026

I keep seeing claims like "95% tungsten is noticeably thinner than 90%." Nobody ever posts actual numbers. So I measured 12 barrels across three tungsten percentages, all at 23g, all torpedo shape, from three different manufacturers.

Here are the results.

The test setup

Digital calipers (Mitutoyo, 0.01mm resolution). Each barrel measured at the widest point of the torpedo profile. Three measurements per barrel, averaged. All barrels are 23g steel tip with points removed for consistency.

What the numbers actually show

80% to 90%: average drop of 0.72mm. That is massive. You can feel this difference with your eyes closed. A 90% barrel at 23g is a fundamentally different grip experience from an 80% barrel at the same weight.

90% to 95%: average drop of 0.28mm. Noticeable if you are sensitive to grip changes, but much less dramatic than the 80-to-90 jump. Most players would struggle to identify this difference in a blind test.

95% to 97%: only one data point (Designa Ultralite at 6.08mm) but the trend continues - another 0.15mm thinner. Diminishing returns are clear.

The practical takeaway

The biggest value jump in darts is from 80% to 90% tungsten. You get roughly three-quarters of a millimetre thinner barrel for a price increase of about ten to fifteen pounds. Going from 90% to 95% costs twenty to thirty pounds more and gets you less than 0.3mm. Going from 95% to 97% costs even more and gets you even less.

For grouping purposes, that 0.72mm difference between 80% and 90% translates to roughly 1.5mm more board space between three tightly grouped darts. At the treble 20 bed (about 10mm wide), that extra space is the difference between three darts fitting and the third one deflecting off the second.

At 90% to 95%, the 0.28mm difference gives you maybe 0.6mm more space. Measurable, but unlikely to change your grouping unless you are already hitting tight trebles consistently.

My recommendation

If you are throwing 80% tungsten and want to improve grouping, upgrade to 90%. It is the single most cost-effective equipment change in darts. If you are already on 90% and wondering about 95%, save your money unless your average is above 70 and you are hitting trebles regularly enough for barrel diameter to be the limiting factor. For most pub and league players, 90% is the ceiling of meaningful returns.
