90% vs 95% Tungsten: Is the Upgrade Worth It?

By TungstenTalk | March 2026

I get asked this constantly. "Should I upgrade from 90% to 95% tungsten?" The answer is almost always no. Here is why, with actual measurements to back it up.

The diameter difference

From my caliper tests across 12 barrels (full data in my tungsten percentage comparison), the average diameter difference between 90% and 95% at 23g is 0.28mm. That is roughly the thickness of two sheets of printer paper.

For context, the treble 20 bed is about 10mm wide. Three darts at 6.5mm (90%) take up 19.5mm. Three at 6.2mm (95%) take up 18.6mm. The difference is 0.9mm of extra board space. Can you feel that? Maybe. Does it change your scoring? Almost certainly not unless you are averaging above 70.

The price difference

90%20-35 pounds~6.5mm
95%45-70 pounds~6.2mm

You pay 2x the price for 0.28mm less diameter. That is terrible value unless the 0.28mm actually matters to your game.

When 95% makes sense

You average 70+ consistently. You group tight enough that barrel diameter is genuinely the limiting factor. You have tried 90% for at least six months and have data showing your grouping is limited by barrel width, not by your throw consistency.

That describes maybe 5% of dart players. The other 95% should spend the price difference on practice time or a better board.

What I actually recommend

Start with 90%. Play with them for a year. If after a year your grouping is consistently tight enough that you are physically running out of space in the treble bed, consider 95%. I found a solid guide on dart barrel shapes at TheDartScout that covers how barrel profile matters more than tungsten percentage for most players.

The shape of the barrel - torpedo vs straight vs bomb - affects your grip, your release, and your flight arc. Tungsten percentage only affects diameter. Shape is a bigger variable than percentage for anyone below county-level standard.

My current setup

I throw 90% tungsten Designa Dark Thunders at 23g. I have tested 95% barrels extensively. I went back to 90% because the diameter difference was not worth the price difference and the 90% barrels are slightly more durable (less prone to chipping on the grip grooves). I would rather spend the extra 30 pounds on flights, shafts, and a decent sharpening stone from Darts Corner.
