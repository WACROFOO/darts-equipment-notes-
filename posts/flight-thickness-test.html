Flight Thickness Test: 75 vs 150 Micron

By TungstenTalk | March 2026

Everyone says thicker flights last longer. Nobody says how much longer. So I ran a test.

Method

Two identical sets of Winmau Prism Alpha flights. One at 75 micron, one at 150 micron. Same shape (standard). Same barrel (Designa Dark Thunder 23g). Same board (Winmau Blade 6). 100 darts per session, noted damage after each.

Results

Metric75 micron150 micron
First visible damageSession 3 (300 darts)Session 9 (900 darts)
First creaseSession 5 (500 darts)Session 14 (1,400 darts)
UnusableSession 8 (800 darts)Session 22 (2,200 darts)
Cost per set75p2.50 pounds
Cost per 1,000 darts94p1.14 pounds

Analysis

150-micron flights lasted 2.75x longer. They cost 3.3x more per set. Cost per dart thrown is nearly identical - the 150s are only 21% more expensive per dart.

The real saving is convenience. With 75-micron, I replaced every 4-5 sessions. With 150-micron, every 11-12 sessions.

What surprised me

The 75-micron flights developed a permanent crease by session 5 that I did not notice until I checked. I had been throwing with compromised flights for three sessions. The 150-micron flights held their 90-degree cross shape until session 14. That is 14 sessions of consistent aerodynamics versus 5.

Recommendation

If you play 3+ times per week, use 150-micron. Durability better, consistency better, cost per session nearly identical. 100-micron is fine for most players. But if you have been using 75s, try 150s for a month.

I bought all test flights from Darts Corner. Winmau publishes micron ratings on their Prism range packaging. The DartsNutz forum has a good thread on flight longevity with dozens of players reporting similar numbers.
