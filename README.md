## Barrels

Barrels are the main body of the dart and the most important factor affecting grip and feel.

| Material | Weight Range | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Tungsten (90%+) | 20-26g | Slim profile, durable, preferred by pros |
| Brass | 16-26g | Budget-friendly, thicker barrel |
| Nickel-Silver | 18-24g | Mid-range option |

**Common barrel shapes:** straight, torpedo, bomb, scalloped

**Grip textures:** smooth, ringed, knurled, shark-cut

---

## Shafts

Shafts (also called stems) connect the barrel to the flight.

| Type | Material | Durability | Notes |
|------|----------|------------|-------|
| Short | Nylon/Polycarbonate | Medium | Less wobble, tighter groupings |
| Medium | Aluminium | High | Popular all-round choice |
| Long | Carbon Fibre | High | More stability for loopy throws |

- **Standard thread:** BA (British Association) — universal across most darts
- **Spin shafts** can reduce deflections by rotating on impact

---

## Flights

Flights stabilise the dart in the air and affect trajectory.

| Shape | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Standard | Most drag, slower arc |
| Slim | Less drag, flatter trajectory |
| Kite | Balanced, good all-round |
| Pear | Moderate drag, popular with beginners |

- **Thickness:** Standard ~100 micron; Thick/Mega flights last longer but weigh more
- **Material:** Polyester (most common), nylon, or moulded polycarbonate

## Dartboards

### Bristle Boards (recommended)
- Made from compressed sisal fibres
- Self-healing — fibres close after dart removal
- Rotate the board regularly to even out wear on the 20 segment

### Electronic Boards
- Plastic segmented surface with small holes
- Soft-tip darts required (usually 14-18g)
- Built-in scoring, good for beginners and multiplayer games

**Board height (bullseye):** 5 ft 8 in (1.73 m) from the floor
**Throwing distance (oche):** 7 ft 9 in (2.37 m) from the face of the board

## Accessories

- **Cases/Wallets** — Hard or soft cases to store and carry darts
- **Dart Tool / Extractor** — Removes stuck darts or broken shafts
- **Shaft Rings (O-rings)** — Prevent shafts from loosening mid-game
- **Point Sharpener** — Keeps steel-tip points sharp for clean entry
- **Lighting** — Dartboard surrounds and ring lights improve visibility and protect walls
- **Surround / Catch Ring** — Foam or rubber surround to protect the wall

---

## Setup Tips

1. Mount the board at the correct height and distance before playing.
2. Rotate the board every few weeks to distribute wear evenly across all segments.
3. Check shaft tightness before every session — loose shafts cause deflections.
4. Break in new flights with a slight fold along the central spine for better spread.
5. Store darts vertically (point-up) to avoid bending shafts or creasing flights.
6. Match weight to your throw — heavier darts suit a slower throw; lighter darts suit a faster release.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Barrel** | The main body of the dart, held during throwing |
| **Shaft / Stem** | The connector between barrel and flight |
| **Flight** | The wing at the back of the dart that provides aerodynamic stability |
| **Oche** | The throwing line |
| **Bullseye** | The centre of the dartboard (25 pts outer bull, 50 pts inner bull) |
| **Treble 20** | The highest-scoring single-dart segment (60 pts) |
| **180** | Maximum score from three darts (three treble 20s) |
| **Checkout** | A combination of darts that ends the game by reaching exactly zero |
| **Double-out** | Standard format requiring the final dart to land in a double segment |
